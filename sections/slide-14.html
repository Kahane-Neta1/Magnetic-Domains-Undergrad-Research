Summary

Different types of changes: thinning, movement, and disappearance, and their proportions as voltage increases. The width histogram shows that width decreases with increasing voltage until a minimum width is reached. Domains disappear in jumps, with partial equilibrium at constant time intervals. The number of disappearing components decreases exponentially, with the same decay constant across different voltage jumps. The most curved domains disappear first, and domains with large surface area are more likely to disappear.
